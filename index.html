The Derivative — Formal Definition

f′(x) = limh→0 [ f(x+h) − f(x) ] / h

For f(x) = x²: f′(x) = 2x

Chain Rule: for g(f(x)), derivative = g′(f(x)) · f′(x).

Integration: ∫x² dx = x³/3 + C
ChouAI Explanation — Plain Language

A derivative tells you how fast something is changing at any instant — like a speedometer for a curve. The formula shrinks the gap between two points to zero to find the exact rate of change.

The Chain Rule handles nested functions. Integration reverses the process — finding total area under a curve, like calculating distance from a speed graph.

Regenerative braking is an electromechanical process that recovers kinetic energy during deceleration and converts it into electrical energy. In railway transport, the traction motor operates as a generator, producing an electromotive force proportional to its rotational speed. This energy, which would otherwise be dissipated as heat, can be stored in supercapacitors and reused during the next acceleration phase. The Process
How ChouAI Works

Three simple steps from document to insight.

1

Upload Your PDF

Drag and drop or click to select any PDF. ChouAI uses PDF.js to read the file entirely in your browser — nothing is ever uploaded to a server. Your document stays 100% private.

2

Choose Your Output

Select a concise Summary, a deep Explanation of key concepts, or Both. For summaries, choose your preferred word count range — from a quick 40–70 word snapshot to a full 150+ word overview.

3

Get Your Results

Your text is sent securely to Qwen2.5-72B via Hugging Face. ChouAI automatically detects the document's language and responds in the same language. Copy or download your results instantly.

⚙ Under the Hood

ChouAI uses PDF.js for in-browser PDF text extraction. AI is powered by facebook/bart-large-cnn via the Hugging Face Inference API. Language detection runs entirely client-side. No backend, no database — a single HTML file hosted on GitHub Pages, free and private by default.
